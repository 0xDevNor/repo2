1. **Generate a PGP Key Pair**: Every user has a pair of keys - a public key and a private key. The public key is shared with others to encrypt messages for you, while the private key is kept secret and used to decrypt messages sent to you.

2. **Encrypting Messages**: When someone wants to send you a secure message, they use your public key to encrypt the message. Once a message is encrypted with your public key, it can only be decrypted with your corresponding private key.

3. **Signing Messages**: To prove that a message genuinely comes from you, you can 'sign' it with your private key. Others can use your public key to verify the signature, confirming the message hasn't been altered and that it indeed comes from you.

4. **Decrypting Messages**: When you receive an encrypted message, you use your private key to decrypt it. If you have the sender's public key, you can also verify if the message was signed, ensuring its authenticity.
